The trade: Shares of AMC at 8.14 on September 14, 2023.

AMC Entertainment Holdings Inc (NYSE: AMC) is a prominent American movie theater chain and entertainment company. As of September 14th, it is currently trading at $8.14, an increase of over 13% in the past 5 days. Although AMC is distressed, I believe that the firm is undervalued because of its recent financial deals and new business model. On Wednesday, AMC successfully raised around $325 million in new equity capital by issuing 40 million shares. This figure adds to the previous $435.3 million in cash and equivalents that the company had reported in the last earnings season – totaling $760 million. This move ends much of the bankruptcy fears shared by investors over the past year. In addition, AMC has also finalized a deal with Taylor Swift to be the sole distributor of her movie “Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour”. This move has already broken box office records by recording over $65 million in box office sales (as of 9/15) and projects up to $1 billion for total revenue generated. Furthermore, AMC’s improved business plan that seeks to target new streams of revenue, such as showing musical concerts, replaying older movies, and offering a subscription service, has allowed the firm to improve its cash flow despite recent events (Hollywood strikes which will hopefully end soon). AMC's second quarter earnings showed revenue rising 16% year over year and so far, its third quarter total revenue is about $2.4 billion – which is already a 21% increase from last year. As competitors such as Regal and Cinemark face difficulties with movie release dates continuing to delay, AMC can take advantage of the entertainment market through exploring new revenue streams. Ultimately, due to AMC’s recent equity raise and new business model, I believe that AMC’s stock will rise in the next few months.
