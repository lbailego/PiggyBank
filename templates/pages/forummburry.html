I recognize the danger posed by millions of low-income borrowers obtaining homes with substantial leverage, often without down payments. Home prices are soaring to extreme heights and the market’s valuations are unaware of the facade, but I see an unsustainable nature in these subprime loans and the housing market as a whole. Thus I am currently debating on the best way to capitalize on my insight. Although this may sound risky, I want to buy credit default swaps against vulnerable subprime deals provided by investment banks. This way, if and when the market crashes, the banks will lose all of their valuable premium and my CD’s will skyrocket. I am very worried, however, if my insight is wrong, I sure am betting a lot of money on this but “Go big or go home”.
