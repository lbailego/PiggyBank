Exxon Mobile Stock, Novemeber 2020.

Posted by Rishi Shah on January 22 2024

Introduction:

My name is Rishi Shah, I am a junior at the Peddie School and have been investing for 3 years. I intend on using investing to compound my money in the long term through both holding and quick trades.


The trade: Exxon Mobil @ $35.00 in November 2020.

In November of 2020, Donald Trump was replaced with Joe Biden, who had vastly different political views. Based on Biden's campaigning for a more green America powered by electricity, it was evident that he was going to attack the use of natural gases. He would do this by enacting policies cutting down the amount of oil the country moves and leases. Because of this, I anticipated, along with many news sources such as Bloomberg, that gas prices would skyrocket. Exxon Mobil is a massive gas corporation in America, so I chose to invest into their stock, expecting it to increase with the rising gas prices. I ended up buying 5 shares of Exxon Mobil. Looking back, I would've bought more if I had more to invest at the time. It is now at $96.90.

My Takeaways:

I had always kept up with the news, but this was the first time the news influenced my stock trading. This experience showed me the importance of being in touch with how current events will affect the markets - sources like Bloomberg, CNBC, and Reuters are all great places to get information. For example, Boeing is struggling with airplane door functional issues right now, causing their stock to take a hit. Keeping up with the news has allowed me to capitalize off this through trading options predicting the price to go down.
